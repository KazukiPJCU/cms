Reflection and Insights

Learning Joomla was a challenging process, as the program initially is very overwhelming. However, with time and practice, I became more comfortable managing the site structure and incorporating extensions to enhance functionality.

Working with Joomla templates helped me appreciate the platform's flexibility and adaptability. While Joomla's learning curve was steeper than WordPress, I found that using this CMS allowed me to tackle more complex web projects confidently.

One issue I had while working with Joomla was editing the CSS files in the template, as it's just a wall of CSS text without any gaps or spacing.

Changing colors on Joomla was also an issue because the website colors wouldn't update sometimes without clearing the browser cache. This caused me to have to troubleshoot the issue.

WordPress, on the other hand, was much more user-friendly when designing and updating a website.

As a beginner, I found the platform's interface intuitive and easy to navigate, making it a breeze to create new pages, posts, and menus.

Creating websites with WordPress was generally quicker and more straightforward than with Joomla, but I found that WordPress's lack of advanced customization meant that I had to look for additional plugins.

Overall, Joomla allowed for more customization at the cost of ease of use, while WordPress enabled me to create a website more efficiently, at the cost of customization.

For my chosen hosting, I used SiteGround and Joomla to host my WordPress CMS website and Joomla CMS website, respectively.

On SiteGround, I had to pay for a server subscription to host my WordPress site, as I had previously created a SiteGround profile and wasn't able to get the student discount. Which cost something on the lines of $45+, an expense I could of done without

Joomla, on the other hand, was completely free and auto-installed Joomla for me to begin creating my website.

At times, the Joomla admin control panel would stop working or not allow me to log in. This issue was fixed by restarting the browser.

Alternatively, SiteGround had a one-click installer for WordPress and was generally easier to navigate and never locked me out of the admin panel.
